# Executive Summary

## Project title

**Production Scheduling Optimization: Genetic Algorithm for Total Tardiness Reduction**

## Summary

This project demonstrates how a robust Genetic Algorithm can be used to minimize total tardiness in identical parallel machine scheduling problems.

## Business problem

Manufacturers need to assign and sequence jobs across machines while meeting due dates. Poor schedules lead to tardiness, late delivery, production instability, and lower customer satisfaction.

## Proposed solution

The project uses a crossover-free Genetic Algorithm with vegetative reproduction, four mutation strategies, expanded initial population, surrogate fitness function, immigration, and elitist selection.

## Key result

The GA showed strong accuracy and robustness across 13 benchmark instances. It consistently reached the optimum in 7 instances and achieved less than 5% average deviation from optimal in 12 instances.

## Consulting relevance

The approach can support scheduling decision-support systems, due-date reliability improvement, production planning, advanced manufacturing analytics, and operations transformation.
